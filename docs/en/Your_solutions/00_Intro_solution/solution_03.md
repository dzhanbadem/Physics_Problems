To determine how the gravitational force changes, we use the method of ratios by comparing the initial force with the new force after the changes are applied.

### 1. The Initial State
The original gravitational force between the two masses is given by:
$$F_{\text{initial}} = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$



### 2. The Final State (Applying Changes)
According to the problem, we apply the following modifications:
* **Masses are halved:** $m_1' = \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_2' = \frac{1}{2}m_2$
* **Distance is doubled:** $r' = 2r$

Now, we substitute these new values into the gravitational formula to find the new force ($F_{\text{new}}$):
$$F_{\text{new}} = G \frac{\left( \frac{1}{2}m_1 \right) \left( \frac{1}{2}m_2 \right)}{(2r)^2}$$

### 3. Step-by-Step Simplification
**Step A: Simplify the numerator**
$$\left( \frac{1}{2}m_1 \right) \left( \frac{1}{2}m_2 \right) = \frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2$$

**Step B: Simplify the denominator**
$$(2r)^2 = 4r^2$$

**Step C: Combine the terms**
$$F_{\text{new}} = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}$$
$$F_{\text{new}} = \left( \frac{1/4}{4} \right) \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$
$$F_{\text{new}} = \frac{1}{16} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$



---

### 4. Conclusion
By comparing the two equations, we can see that:
$$F_{\text{new}} = \frac{1}{16} F_{\text{initial}}$$

**Result:** The gravitational force decreases by a factor of **16**. 

**Physics Insight:** * Halving both masses reduces the force to **1/4** of its original value (direct proportionality to the product of masses).
* Doubling the distance reduces the force to **1/4** of its original value (inverse square law: $1/2^2 = 1/4$).
* Combined, these effects multiply: $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16}$.
