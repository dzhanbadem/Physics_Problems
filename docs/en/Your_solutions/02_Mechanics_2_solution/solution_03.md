To solve this, we will use the **Law of Conservation of Energy**. We assume there is no air resistance, meaning the potential energy at the release point converts entirely into kinetic energy at the bottom.

### 1. The "Given" & "Find"
* **Given Length ($L$):** $1.0\text{ m}$
* **Initial Angle ($\theta$):** $15^\circ$
* **Gravity ($g$):** $9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
* **Find:** Velocity at the bottom ($v$)

---

### 2. The Concept
As the bob hangs at an angle, it is lifted to a certain height ($h$) above its lowest point.
* **At the start:** The bob has Maximum Gravitational Potential Energy ($PE = mgh$).
* **At the bottom:** All that potential energy has turned into Kinetic Energy ($KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$).



By setting $PE = KE$, we get:
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$
Notice that mass ($m$) cancels out, leaving:
$$v = \sqrt{2gh}$$

---

### 3. The Setup: Finding the Height ($h$)
To find $v$, we first need the vertical height $h$. Using trigonometry, the vertical distance from the pivot to the bob at an angle is $L \cos(\theta)$. Therefore, the height $h$ is the total length minus that vertical distance:
$$h = L - L \cos(\theta) = L(1 - \cos\theta)$$

---

### 4. The Calculation
**Step 1: Calculate $h$.**
Using $\theta = 15^\circ$ and $L = 1.0$:
$$h = 1.0 \times (1 - \cos 15^\circ)$$
$$\cos 15^\circ \approx 0.9659$$
$$h = 1.0 \times (1 - 0.9659) = 0.0341\text{ m}$$

**Step 2: Calculate $v$.**
Now, plug $h$ and $g$ into the velocity formula:
$$v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 0.0341}$$
$$v = \sqrt{0.66836}$$
$$v \approx 0.817\text{ m/s}$$

---

### 5. The Result
The speed of the bob at the bottom of the swing is approximately **$0.82\text{ m/s}$**.

**Teaching Tip:** Point out to your class that the **mass of the bob does not matter**. Whether it's a marble or a bowling ball, if the length and angle are the same, they will reach the same speed at the bottom!
