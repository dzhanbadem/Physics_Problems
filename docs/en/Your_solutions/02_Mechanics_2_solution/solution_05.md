This problem is an example of a **perfectly inelastic collision**, which occurs when two objects stick together after impact and move with a common final velocity.

### 1. The "Given" & "Find"
* **Runner ($m_1$):** $70\text{ kg}$
* **Initial Speed of Runner ($v_1$):** $3\text{ m/s}$
* **Cart ($m_2$):** $140\text{ kg}$
* **Initial Speed of Cart ($v_2$):** $0\text{ m/s}$
* **Find:** Final speed ($v_f$) and check for Kinetic Energy conservation.

---

### 2. Part A: Final Speed (Momentum)
**The Concept:**
In any isolated system, total momentum before the collision equals total momentum after the collision. Since they move together, we add their masses for the final state.



**The Setup:**
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

**The Calculation:**
1.  **Plug in values:** $(70 \times 3) + (140 \times 0) = (70 + 140)v_f$
2.  **Simplify:** $210 + 0 = 210v_f$
3.  **Solve:** $v_f = \frac{210}{210} = 1\text{ m/s}$

The final speed of the cart with the runner is **$1\text{ m/s}$**.

---

### 3. Part B: Kinetic Energy Conservation
**The Concept:**
To see if Kinetic Energy ($KE$) is conserved, we compare the total $KE$ before the jump to the total $KE$ after the jump.

**The Setup:**
$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

**The Calculation:**
1.  **Initial $KE$ (Runner only):**
    $$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3)^2 = 35 \times 9 = 315\text{ J}$$
2.  **Final $KE$ (Combined):**
    $$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(70 + 140)(1)^2 = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1) = 105\text{ J}$$

---

### 4. The Result & Explanation
* **Final Speed:** **$1\text{ m/s}$**
* **Is $KE$ conserved?** **No.**

**Explanation for your class:**
Kinetic energy is **not conserved** in this collision. We started with $315\text{ J}$ and ended with only $105\text{ J}$, meaning $210\text{ J}$ of energy was "lost." 

In physics, this energy isn't actually gone; it was transformed into other forms, such as **internal energy** (vibrations in the cart), **sound** (the thud of the runner landing), and **heat** generated by the friction between the runner’s shoes and the cart deck.

**Teaching Tip:** Remind the class that while *momentum* is always conserved in collisions, *kinetic energy* is only conserved in "elastic" collisions (like two billiard balls bouncing off each other).
