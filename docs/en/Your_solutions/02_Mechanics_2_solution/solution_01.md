For the second part of your presentation, we will shift from comparing two environments to finding a specific physical dimension (length) based on a desired outcome (a 1-second period).

### 1. The "Given" & "Find"
* **Given:** Period ($T$) = $1\text{ s}$
* **Given:** Gravity on Earth ($g$) $\approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$
* **Find:** Length of the pendulum ($L$)

---

### 2. The Concept
We use the standard period formula for a simple pendulum. To find the length, we need to rearrange the formula to solve for $L$:

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$



---

### 3. The Setup (Algebraic Derivation)
To isolate $L$, we follow these algebraic steps:

1.  **Divide by $2\pi$:**
    $$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$
2.  **Square both sides** to remove the square root:
    $$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$
3.  **Multiply by $g$** to solve for $L$:
    $$L = g \left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2$$

---

### 4. The Calculation
Now, we plug in our known values ($T = 1$ and $g = 9.8$):

**Step 1: Plug in the numbers.**
$$L = 9.8 \times \left(\frac{1}{2\pi}\right)^2$$

**Step 2: Calculate the denominator.**
Since $\pi \approx 3.14159$, then $2\pi \approx 6.283$.
$$(6.283)^2 \approx 39.478$$

**Step 3: Final Division.**
$$L = \frac{9.8}{39.478}$$
$$L \approx 0.2482\text{ m}$$

---

### 5. The Result
The required length is approximately **0.248 meters**, or **24.8 cm**.

> **Note for your class:** A pendulum with a period of 2 seconds (1 second for each swing direction) is known as a **Seconds Pendulum**. Its length is roughly 1 meter. Since our target period is half of that (1 second), the length must be significantly shorter because $L$ is proportional to $T^2$.
