This problem is a classic "two-parter." First, we use energy to find the speed before the collision, and then we use momentum to find the speed after they stick together.

### 1. The "Given" & "Find"
* **Block A ($m_1$):** $0.5\text{ kg}$
* **Initial Height ($h$):** $3.0\text{ m}$
* **Block B ($m_2$):** $1.5\text{ kg}$ (at rest)
* **Find:** Final velocity of the combined mass ($v_f$)

---

### 2. The Concept
This problem involves two distinct physics principles:
1.  **Conservation of Mechanical Energy:** To find how fast Block A is moving when it reaches the bottom of the track.
2.  **Conservation of Linear Momentum:** To find the velocity after the **inelastic collision** (where the objects stick together).



---

### 3. Step 1: Speed at the bottom (Energy)
Before the collision, all of Block A's potential energy ($PE$) at the top becomes kinetic energy ($KE$) at the bottom.
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

**The Calculation:**
1.  Cancel the mass ($m$) and solve for $v$: $v = \sqrt{2gh}$
2.  Plug in the values: $v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 3.0}$
3.  $v = \sqrt{58.8} \approx 7.67\text{ m/s}$

So, Block A hits Block B at **$7.67\text{ m/s}$**.

---

### 4. Step 2: The Collision (Momentum)
In a collision where objects stick together, momentum is conserved:
$$m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

**The Calculation:**
1.  **Identify values:** $m_1 = 0.5$, $v_1 = 7.67$, $m_2 = 1.5$, $v_2 = 0$ (since it was at rest).
2.  **Set up the equation:**
    $$(0.5 \times 7.67) + (1.5 \times 0) = (0.5 + 1.5)v_f$$
3.  **Simplify:**
    $$3.835 = 2.0 \times v_f$$
4.  **Solve for $v_f$:**
    $$v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0} = 1.9175\text{ m/s}$$

---

### 5. The Result
The speed of the combined mass just after the collision is approximately **$1.92\text{ m/s}$**.

**Teaching Tip:** Explain to the class why we didn't just use Conservation of Energy for the whole problem. In an **inelastic collision** (sticking together), kinetic energy is actually lost to heat and sound, so only **momentum** is conserved during the impact!
