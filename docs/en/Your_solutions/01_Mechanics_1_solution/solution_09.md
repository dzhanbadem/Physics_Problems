To determine which object has greater momentum, we follow a standard three-step process: defining the physical quantity, converting units to the International System (SI), and performing the calculation.

---

### 1. Definition of Momentum
Momentum ($\vec{p}$) is a vector quantity defined as the product of an object's mass ($m$) and its velocity ($\vec{v}$). 
$$p = m \times v$$
In the SI system, mass must be in **kilograms (kg)** and velocity in **meters per second (m/s)**. The resulting unit for momentum is $\text{kg} \cdot \text{m/s}$.



---

### 2. Step 1: Calculate the Fly's Momentum
**Given:**
* Mass ($m_{fly}$) = $2 \text{ g}$
* Velocity ($v_{fly}$) = $10 \text{ m/s}$

**Conversion:**
Since $1000 \text{ g} = 1 \text{ kg}$, we convert the mass:
$$m_{fly} = \frac{2}{1000} \text{ kg} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$$

**Calculation:**
$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \text{ kg} \times 10 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_{fly} = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}}$$

---

### 3. Step 2: Calculate the Tennis Ball's Momentum
**Given:**
* Mass ($m_{ball}$) = $60 \text{ g}$
* Velocity ($v_{ball}$) = $1 \text{ m/s}$

**Conversion:**
Convert the mass to kilograms:
$$m_{ball} = \frac{60}{1000} \text{ kg} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$$

**Calculation:**
$$p_{ball} = 0.06 \text{ kg} \times 1 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_{ball} = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}}$$

---

### 4. Step 3: Comparison and Interpretation
Now we compare the two results:
* **Fly:** $0.02 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}$
* **Tennis Ball:** $0.06 \text{ kg} \cdot \text{m/s}$

$$0.06 > 0.02$$

**Conclusion:**
The **tennis ball** has greater momentum. 

**Physics Insight for your presentation:** Even though the fly is moving **10 times faster** than the tennis ball, the tennis ball is **30 times heavier**. Because momentum depends linearly on both mass and velocity, the much larger mass of the tennis ball outweighs the higher speed of the fly in this comparison.
