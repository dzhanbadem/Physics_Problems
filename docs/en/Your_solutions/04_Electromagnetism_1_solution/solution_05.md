This problem is a great way to introduce the concept of **balancing forces** from different fields—in this case, the Gravitational Field and the Electric Field.

---

## Problem Breakdown: Proton Levitation

### 1. The Concept
For an object to "levitate," the net force acting on it must be zero. There are two vertical forces acting on the proton:
*   **Gravity ($F_g$):** Pulling the proton downward toward the Earth.
*   **Electric Force ($F_e$):** Pushing the proton upward.

To achieve equilibrium:
$$F_e = F_g$$



### 2. The Formulas
We define these two forces using the following equations:
*   **Gravitational Force:** $F_g = m \cdot g$
*   **Electric Force:** $F_e = q \cdot E$ (where $E$ is the electric field strength)

By setting them equal, we get the master equation for levitation:
$$qE = mg$$

### 3. The Variables
*   **Mass ($m$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
*   **Charge ($q$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
*   **Gravity ($g$):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
*   **Electric Field ($E$):** Unknown (Target variable)

### 4. Step-by-Step Calculation
First, we rearrange the equation to solve for $E$:
$$E = \frac{mg}{q}$$

Now, plug in the values:
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \cdot (9.8 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$

Calculate the numerator (the weight of the proton):
$$Weight \approx 1.6366 \times 10^{-26} \text{ N}$$

Divide by the charge:
$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ V/m (or N/C)}$$

### 5. Final Result
*   **Electric Field Strength:** $1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$
*   **Direction:** **Upward** (Since the proton is positive, the field must point upward to apply an upward force).

---

### Logic Check for your Class:
Ask the students if this field strength seems large or small. For context, a typical "static electricity" spark from a doorknob involves fields around $3 \times 10^6 \text{ N/C}$. 

The field required to lift a proton is **incredibly small** ($0.000000102 \text{ N/C}$). This reinforces the lesson from the previous problem: at the subatomic level, gravity is so weak that even a microscopic electric field is enough to completely overcome it.
