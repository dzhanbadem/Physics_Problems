## Problem Breakdown: Force on a Wire

### 1. The Concept
When an electric current (which is just a flow of many charges) passes through a magnetic field, each individual charge experiences a Lorentz force. Collectively, these forces result in a macroscopic force on the wire itself.

The formula for this force is:
$$F = I L B \sin(\theta)$$
*   **$I$** is the current in Amperes (A).
*   **$L$** is the length of the wire in meters (m).
*   **$B$** is the magnetic field strength in Tesla (T).
*   **$\theta$** is the angle between the direction of the current and the magnetic field.



### 2. The Variables
*   **Length ($L$):** $2.0 \text{ m}$
*   **Current ($I$):** $10 \text{ A}$
*   **Magnetic Field ($B$):** $0.5 \text{ T}$

First, let's calculate the "base" product ($I \cdot L \cdot B$):
$$10 \text{ A} \cdot 2.0 \text{ m} \cdot 0.5 \text{ T} = 10 \text{ N}$$

### 3. Calculations for Different Angles

#### a) Angle = $90^\circ$ (Perpendicular)
The sine of $90^\circ$ is $1$.
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(90^\circ) = 10 \cdot 1 = \mathbf{10 \text{ N}}$$
*This is the maximum possible force for this setup.*

#### b) Angle = $45^\circ$
The sine of $45^\circ$ is $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707$.
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(45^\circ) = 10 \cdot 0.707 = \mathbf{7.07 \text{ N}}$$

#### c) Angle = $0^\circ$ (Parallel)
The sine of $0^\circ$ is $0$.
$$F = 10 \cdot \sin(0^\circ) = 10 \cdot 0 = \mathbf{0 \text{ N}}$$

---

### 4. Summary and Presentation Logic
*   **Maximum Force ($90^\circ$):** When the wire cuts directly across the field lines.
*   **Zero Force ($0^\circ$):** When the wire runs parallel to the field lines. The magnetic field cannot "grip" the charges if they are moving in the same direction as the field.

**Key takeaway for your class:**
You can explain that this is why wires in a motor are wound into coils. By changing the orientation of the wire relative to the magnets, we can control the amount of force (torque) generated, allowing the motor to spin.
