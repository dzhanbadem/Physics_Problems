This is a classic problem for demonstrating the **principle of superposition**. Since force is a vector quantity, we don't just add the numbers; we have to look at the geometry and direction of each pull.

---

## Problem Breakdown: Four Charges on a Square

### 1. The Concept
According to **Coulomb's Law**, the force between two point charges is proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. 
$$F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
Because the central charge is negative ($-2.0$ C) and the corner charges are positive ($+1.0$ C), the forces will all be **attractive**. Each corner charge will try to "pull" the center charge toward itself.

### 2. The Geometry (Finding $r$)
To find the force, we first need the distance ($r$) from any corner to the center of the square.
*   The diagonal of a square with side $s = 1.0$ m is $s\sqrt{2} = \sqrt{2}$ m.
*   The distance from a corner to the center is half of the diagonal:
$$r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \text{ m}$$
*   Therefore, $r^2 = (\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})^2 = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5 \text{ m}^2$.



### 3. Magnitude of Individual Forces
Since all corner charges are identical ($1.0$ C) and equidistant from the center, the magnitude of the force ($F$) exerted by **each** corner charge is the same:
*   $k \approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
*   $q_1 = 1.0 \text{ C}$
*   $q_2 = 2.0 \text{ C}$ (magnitude)
*   $r^2 = 0.5 \text{ m}^2$

$$F = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9)(1.0)(2.0)}{0.5} = 3.596 \times 10^{10} \text{ N}$$

### 4. Vector Addition (The Solution)
Now we look at the directions:
*   The charge at the **Top-Left** pulls the center charge "Up and Left."
*   The charge at the **Bottom-Right** pulls the center charge "Down and Right."
*   The charge at the **Top-Right** pulls the center charge "Up and Right."
*   The charge at the **Bottom-Left** pulls the center charge "Down and Left."

Because the square is perfectly symmetrical and the charges are identical:
1.  The pull from the Top-Left is **exactly cancelled** by the pull from the Bottom-Right.
2.  The pull from the Top-Right is **exactly cancelled** by the pull from the Bottom-Left.

### 5. Final Result
*   **Magnitude:** $0 \text{ N}$
*   **Direction:** N/A (The charge is in static equilibrium)

> **Logic Check:** In any system where identical charges are placed symmetrically around a central point, the net electric force at that center point will always be zero. The "tugs-of-war" happening along the diagonals end in a perfect stalemate.
