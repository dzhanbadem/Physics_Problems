This problem is a fantastic way to show your class the sheer scale of difference between the fundamental forces of nature. We are comparing the "strength" of electricity versus gravity at the atomic level.

---

## Problem Breakdown: Electric vs. Gravitational Force

### 1. The Constants and Variables
First, we need the physical constants for an electron and a proton. 

| Variable | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Proton Charge ($q_p$)** | $+1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$ |
| **Electron Charge ($q_e$)** | $-1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$ |
| **Proton Mass ($m_p$)** | $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$ |
| **Electron Mass ($m_e$)** | $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$ |
| **Distance ($r$)** | $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$ |
| **Coulomb Constant ($k$)** | $8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$ |
| **Gravitational Constant ($G$)** | $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$ |

---

### 2. Calculating Electric Force ($F_e$)
Using Coulomb’s Law:
$$F_e = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$

Substituting the values:
$$F_e = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9) \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e \approx 8.22 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$

---

### 3. Calculating Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
Using Newton’s Law of Universal Gravitation:
$$F_g = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

Substituting the values:
$$F_g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (1.67 \times 10^{-27}) \times (9.11 \times 10^{-31})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g \approx 3.61 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$

---

### 4. The Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)
To see how many times stronger the electric force is, we divide them:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{8.22 \times 10^{-8}}{3.61 \times 10^{-47}}$$
$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$$



---

### 5. Summary and Logic Check
*   **Electric Force:** $\approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$
*   **Gravitational Force:** $\approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$
*   **The Ratio:** $\approx 10^{39}$

**Presentation Insight:**
Tell your class that $10^{39}$ is a number so large it is almost impossible to visualize. To give them a "step-by-step" perspective: if the gravitational force between them was the thickness of a single piece of paper, the electric force would be wider than the entire observable universe. 

This explains why, in chemistry and atomic physics, we **completely ignore gravity**. It is simply too weak to matter when charges are involved.
