This problem is a classic application of the distance formula, but with a "travel" twist that often trips people up in exams.

## 5. Echo Ranging: The Reflection Principle

### The Concept
When you hear an echo, the sound wave has to perform a "round trip." It travels from your mouth to the cliff, reflects off the surface, and travels all the way back to your ears. 

The key for your presentation is to emphasize that the **total time** ($t_{total} = 1\text{ s}$) represents the time to cover **twice the distance** ($2d$) to the cliff.



---

### The Setup
* **Total time ($t$):** $1\text{ s}$
* **Speed of sound ($v$):** $343\text{ m/s}$
* **Target:** Distance to the cliff ($d$)

---

### Step-by-Step Execution

**1. State the basic distance formula:**
In its simplest form, distance equals speed multiplied by time:
$$\text{Total Distance} = v \times t$$

**2. Account for the round trip:**
Since the sound travels to the cliff and back, the total distance is $2d$.
$$2d = v \times t$$

**3. Rearrange to solve for $d$:**
$$d = \frac{v \times t}{2}$$

**4. Substitute the values:**
$$d = \frac{343\text{ m/s} \times 1\text{ s}}{2}$$
$$d = \frac{343\text{ m}}{2}$$

**5. Calculate the final result:**
$$d = 171.5\text{ m}$$

---

### Summary for Presentation
* **Total distance traveled by sound:** $343\text{ m}$
* **Distance to the cliff:** $171.5\text{ m}$

> **Pro-Tip for Class:** Always remind your classmates to "divide by two" in echo problems. A common mistake is to assume the cliff is $343\text{ m}$ away. If it were, the echo would actually take $2\text{ s}$ to return! This same logic is how SONAR and medical ultrasounds calculate depth and distance.
