This problem focuses on **standing wave patterns** and how to visualize the relationship between the physical length of the string and the wave's geometry.

## 7. Standing Wave Modes: The Fourth Harmonic

### The Concept
When a string is fixed at both ends, the standing wave patterns are defined by the number of "loops" or **antinodes**. 
* One antinode (the fundamental) represents half a wavelength ($\frac{1}{2}\lambda$).
* Two antinodes represent one full wavelength ($1\lambda$).
* Therefore, **four antinodes** represent two full wavelengths ($2\lambda$).



---

### The Setup
* **Length of string ($L$):** $80\text{ cm} = 0.8\text{ m}$
* **Number of antinodes ($n$):** $4$
* **Target:** Wavelength ($\lambda$)

---

### Step-by-Step Execution

**1. Determine the Harmonic Relationship:**
For a string fixed at both ends, the length $L$ and the wavelength $\lambda$ are related by:
$$L = n \left( \frac{\lambda}{2} \right)$$
Where $n$ is the number of antinodes (harmonics).

**2. Substitute the known values:**
Since $n = 4$:
$$0.8\text{ m} = 4 \left( \frac{\lambda}{2} \right)$$

**3. Simplify the equation:**
$$0.8\text{ m} = 2\lambda$$

**4. Solve for $\lambda$:**
$$\lambda = \frac{0.8\text{ m}}{2}$$
$$\lambda = 0.4\text{ m}$$

---

### Summary for Presentation
* **String Length:** $0.8\text{ m}$
* **Pattern:** 4 antinodes (2 full waves)
* **Wavelength:** $0.4\text{ m}$ (or $40\text{ cm}$)

> **Pro-Tip for Class:** To help the class visualize this, tell them to imagine the string is divided into 4 equal segments. Each segment is an antinode. Since one full wave requires two segments (one crest and one trough), the wavelength is simply the length of two of those segments: $20\text{ cm} + 20\text{ cm} = 40\text{ cm}$.
